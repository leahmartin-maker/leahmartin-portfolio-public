# Sea Creature Facts for TurtleExplorer

## Sharks
- Sharks are the main natural predator of adult sea turtles.
- Tiger sharks are known to eat sea turtles, using their strong jaws to crush shells.

## Jellyfish
- Jellyfish are a favorite food of leatherback turtles.
- Some jellyfish can sting, but sea turtles are immune to most stings.
- Jellyfish populations can increase when their predators (like turtles) decline.

## Coral
- Coral reefs provide shelter and food for many sea creatures, including young turtles.
- Healthy reefs are important for turtle survival, offering protection from predators.
- Coral is made up of thousands of tiny animals called polyps.

## Algae & Seagrass
- Green sea turtles eat seagrass and algae, helping keep seagrass beds healthy.
- Seagrass beds are nurseries for many ocean animals.
- Algae can grow on turtle shells, sometimes making them look green.

## Killer Whales (Orcas)
- Killer whales are rare but powerful predators of sea turtles, especially in colder waters.
- Orcas use teamwork to hunt and can flip turtles to eat their softer undersides.

## Birds
- Birds like gulls and frigatebirds prey on hatchling turtles as they cross the beach.
- Many hatchlings are eaten before reaching the ocean, but this is part of the natural cycle.

## Raccoons
- Raccoons are a major threat to turtle eggs and hatchlings on nesting beaches.
- They dig up nests to eat eggs, especially where humans have disturbed natural habitats.

## Crabs
- Ghost crabs and other beach crabs eat turtle hatchlings and sometimes eggs.
- Crabs are both predators and important scavengers in the beach ecosystem.

## Fish
- Large fish (like groupers and snappers) may eat hatchling turtles in shallow water.
- Most adult turtles are too big for fish to eat.
