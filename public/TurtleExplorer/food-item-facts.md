# Sea Turtle Food Item Facts – TurtleExplorer

## Jellyfish
- Leatherback turtles eat almost only jellyfish—sometimes hundreds a day!
- Jellyfish are mostly water, so turtles need to eat a lot to get enough energy.
- Eating jellyfish helps keep their populations in balance.

## Seagrass
- Green turtles are the only sea turtles that eat mostly seagrass and algae as adults.
- Grazing turtles help keep seagrass beds healthy and productive.
- Seagrass beds are important nurseries for many ocean animals.

## Crabs
- Kemp’s Ridley turtles love to eat crabs, especially blue crabs in the Gulf of Mexico.
- Crabs are a rich source of protein and energy for turtles.

## Algae
- Young green turtles eat algae before switching to seagrass as adults.
- Algae can grow on rocks, coral, and even turtle shells!

## Mollusks (Clams, Snails)
- Some turtles eat clams and snails, crushing their shells with strong jaws.
- Mollusks are a good source of calcium and nutrients.

## Fish
- Some sea turtles, especially as juveniles, will eat small fish.
- Fish are not a main food for most adult turtles, but are important for young ones.

## Sea Urchins
- Occasionally eaten by some turtles, especially in coral reef areas.
- Sea urchins have spines, but turtles can eat them safely.
