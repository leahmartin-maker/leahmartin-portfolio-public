# Sea Turtle Migration & Species Facts

## Kemp’s Ridley
- **Migration Route:** Gulf of Mexico to Atlantic coast of the US; nests mainly at Rancho Nuevo, Mexico, and Padre Island, Texas (including Corpus Christi).
- **Nesting Site:** Padre Island National Seashore, Texas; Rancho Nuevo, Mexico.
- **Water Temp Range:** 22–28°C (72–82°F) preferred for migration.
- **Food:** Crabs, clams, jellyfish, fish, and some seaweed.
- **Predators:** Sharks, large fish, raccoons (eggs/hatchlings), birds (hatchlings).
- **Unique Facts:**
  - Smallest and most endangered sea turtle.
  - Known for “arribada” mass nesting events.
  - Most nestings in daylight.
  - Corpus Christi is a key release/nesting site.

## Green Turtle
- **Migration Route:** Tropical/subtropical coasts worldwide; migrates between feeding grounds and nesting beaches (Caribbean, Florida, Gulf of Mexico, South Texas).
- **Nesting Site:** South Padre Island, Texas; Florida; Caribbean.
- **Water Temp Range:** 20–28°C (68–82°F).
- **Food:** Seagrass, algae (adults are mostly herbivores), jellyfish (juveniles).
- **Predators:** Sharks, large fish, raccoons (eggs/hatchlings), birds (hatchlings).
- **Unique Facts:**
  - Named for green fat, not shell color.
  - Can hold breath for hours while resting.
  - Grazing helps keep seagrass beds healthy.

## Leatherback
- **Migration Route:** Longest migrations—Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans; nests in Caribbean, South America, and sometimes Gulf of Mexico.
- **Nesting Site:** Caribbean, South America, rarely Texas.
- **Water Temp Range:** 15–30°C (59–86°F); can tolerate colder water than other turtles.
- **Food:** Jellyfish (main diet), tunicates, soft-bodied animals.
- **Predators:** Sharks, killer whales, birds (hatchlings), fish (hatchlings).
- **Unique Facts:**
  - Largest sea turtle (up to 2,000 lbs).
  - Only sea turtle with a soft, leathery shell.
  - Can dive over 1,000 meters (3,000+ feet).
  - Feeds almost exclusively on jellyfish.
